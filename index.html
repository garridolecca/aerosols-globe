About

Real aerosol transport from NASA GEOS-FP (Forward Processing) model. Wind vectors (U10M, V10M) are weighted by each aerosol species' surface mass concentration — particles flow where aerosols actually travel, dense where concentration is high, absent where air is clean.

Data Pipeline

Source: GEOS-FP 0.25° via OPeNDAP (no auth)
Wind: U10M, V10M (10m wind components)
Aerosols: BCSMASS, DUSMASS, SSSMASS, OCSMASS, SO4SMASS
Processing: Wind × normalized(aerosol concentration)
Encoding: U→R, V→G, Magnitude→B in PNG

Earth in Action Aerosols

An animated visualization of real atmospheric aerosol transport using NASA satellite data and global wind patterns.

How This Map Was Created

  1. 1Data Acquisition — Wind vectors (U10M, V10M) and aerosol surface mass concentrations are downloaded in real-time from NASA GEOS-FP (Forward Processing) at 0.25° global resolution via OPeNDAP. No authentication required
  2. 2Aerosol Species — Five species are extracted: BCSMASS Black Carbon, DUSMASS Mineral Dust, SSSMASS Sea Salt, OCSMASS Organic Carbon, SO4SMASS Sulfate.
  3. 3Aerosol Flux Computation — Wind vectors are weighted by each species' normalized concentration: flux = wind × (aerosol / P95). This preserves wind direction but scales magnitude by how much aerosol is actually present.
  4. 4PNG Encoding — U and V components are encoded as Red and Green channels (0–255 mapping to [-max, +max]), with magnitude in Blue. Result: ~100 KB per species at 1° resolution.
  5. 5Browser Rendering — A custom Canvas particle system decodes the PNG, spawns thousands of particles, and advects them along the aerosol flux vectors each frame at 60 FPS.

Data Sources

  • Wind & Aerosols: NASA GMAO GEOS-FP 0.25° — opendap.nccs.nasa.gov
  • Basemap: Esri Dark Gray Vector — ArcGIS Maps SDK 5.0
  • Processing: Python (xarray, NumPy, Pillow) — OPeNDAP streaming

How to Use

  • Switch species — Click any aerosol type in the left panel to see its unique transport pattern
  • Pan & zoom — Drag the map or scroll to explore regions. Particles re-spawn to fill the view.
  • Adjust animation — Use sliders to control particle count, speed, trail length, and line width
  • Interpret colors — Brighter, denser particle trails = higher aerosol concentration being transported by wind
